Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 28, 2026

MyRentLog ("we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how your information is collected, used, and safeguarded when you use our mobile software application (the "Application").

We believe in absolute data transparency. While MyRentLog utilizes cloud infrastructure to ensure your data is securely synced and accessible, your data remains your personal property, and we implement strict industry standards to protect it.

1. Information Collection and Use

A. Personal Data Provided by You

The Application allows you to log, store, and manage your tenancy history, including but not limited to:

  • Tenancy agreements, property details (house, flat, studio, or general property details), and landlord contact information.
  • Logs of rent payments, utility bills, and security deposit receipts.
  • Timelines of communication, photos of property conditions, and official documents (such as Section 8 notices).

B. Cloud Database Architecture (Google Firebase)

To provide seamless synchronization, data backup, and cross-device functionality, MyRentLog utilizes Google Firebase, a cloud-based infrastructure service provided by Google LLC.

Data Transmission and Storage: All personal data, files, photos, and logs that you enter into MyRentLog are securely transmitted and stored on cloud servers managed by Firebase. This infrastructure ensures your records are preserved even if you lose or damage your physical device.

Encryption: Data transmitted between your device and Firebase is encrypted in transit using secure protocols (HTTPS/TLS) and is encrypted at rest on Firebase servers.

No Data Brokerage: We do not track, profile, harvest, analyze, or sell your personal tenancy data or cloud-stored files to advertising networks, insurance companies, landlords, or credit agencies. Your data is used strictly to power the functionality of the Application.

2. Account Security and User Responsibility

A. Authentication and Access

Access to your cloud-synchronized rental history is tied directly to your user account credentials (e.g., your email, password, or third-party authentication provider) managed through Firebase Authentication.

3. Third-Party Services and Analytics

A. App Store and Platform Providers

The Application is distributed through the Apple App Store. When you download, update, or make in-app purchases through the platform, Apple may collect certain telemetry and financial data subject to their own privacy policies. We do not have access to your private financial transaction methods.

B. Firebase Telemetry and Crash Reporting

We may utilize Firebase Crashlytics or Firebase Analytics to monitor application performance, log system errors, and improve software stability. These logs gather anonymized, aggregated technical information (such as device models, operating system versions, and app performance metrics) and do not contain your private names, addresses, financial inputs, or uploaded tenancy documents.

4. Children’s Privacy

The Application is intended for use by individuals who are of legal age to enter into rental contracts and tenancy agreements. We do not knowingly collect or solicit personal data from anyone under the age of 13.

5. Your Data Rights (GDPR, UK Data Protection Act, and CCPA Compliance)

Under applicable privacy laws, you hold full autonomous rights over the data stored within your Firebase account:

  • Access and Portability: You can access all your stored tenancy history directly through the Application interface.
  • Rectification: You can modify or update your records at any time within the app.
  • Erasure: You can delete individual entries at any time. If you wish to completely wipe your entire digital footprint and close your account, you can do so via the in-app account deletion tool, which will permanently purge your data from our Firebase database systems.

We will notify you of any changes by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this document. Your continued use of the Application after a revision constitutes your acceptance of the updated terms.
